# Privacy Policy

**FastLearners App**

**Effective Date: 10 April 2026**

#### **1\. Introduction**

FastLearners Limited (“FastLearners”, “we”, “us”, or “our”) provides digital learning tools, educational content, and online schooling services to students, parents/guardians, teachers, and educational institutions across Nigeria and other West African countries (including Ghana, Sierra Leone, Gambia, and Liberia).

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, store, and protect your personal data when you use our website, mobile app, or any related services (collectively, the “Platform”). We are committed to protecting your privacy and complying with the **Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023 (NDPA)** and all applicable directives issued by the **Nigeria Data Protection Commission (NDPC)**.

We encourage you to read this Policy carefully. If you are a parent or guardian of a child under 18, please also review our **\[Children’s Data Protection Policy\]**.

#### **2\. Information We Collect**

We collect the following categories of personal data:

**a. Account Information**

* Full name  
* Username  
* Email address  
* Phone number (optional)  
* Password (stored in hashed/encrypted form)  
* Gender

**b. Educational Profile**

* Class/grade level  
* School name or institution  
* Subjects studied, quiz/test performance, progress analytics, and learning activity data

**c. Usage and Technical Data**

* App interactions and learning activity logs  
* Device information, IP address, browser type, and timestamps  
* Log files and analytics data

**d. Payment Information**

* Third-party payment transaction references (we do **not** store credit/debit card details or sensitive payment card information).

**e. Children’s Data**

When a child under 18 uses the Platform, we collect the same categories above on their behalf. Parents/guardians can view and manage their child’s profile.

We do not collect sensitive personal data (e.g., health, religion, or biometric data) unless strictly necessary for a specific educational feature and with explicit consent.

#### **3\. Lawful Bases for Processing Personal Data**

Under the NDPA, we process your personal data on the following lawful bases:

* **Performance of a contract** — to provide you with access to the Platform and educational services.  
* **Legitimate interests** — to improve our services, personalise learning experiences, conduct analytics, and ensure platform security.  
* **Consent** — where required, especially for processing children’s data, direct marketing, or optional features.  
* **Legal obligation** — to comply with applicable laws and regulatory requirements.

For children under 18, we rely primarily on **verifiable parental/guardian consent** as required by Section 31 of the NDPA and GAID 2025\.

#### **4\. How We Use Your Personal Data**

We use the collected data to:

* Create and manage user accounts  
* Deliver personalised learning content and track academic progress  
* Analyse usage to improve the Platform and user experience  
* Provide customer support and respond to inquiries  
* Process payments and generate transaction records  
* Comply with legal and regulatory obligations (including NDPC requirements)  
* Detect and prevent fraud, cheating, or misuse of the Platform

We **do not sell, trade, rent, or commercially exploit** your personal data for marketing purposes.

#### **5\. Children’s Data**

We take special care when processing data of children under 18 years of age. We only collect and process such data with verifiable parental or legal guardian consent. Parents/guardians have the right to:

* Access their child’s data  
* Request correction or deletion  
* Withdraw consent (which may limit or terminate the child’s access)

For more details, please see our dedicated **\[Children’s Data Protection Policy\]**.

#### **6\. Data Sharing and Third Parties**

We may share personal data with trusted third parties only when necessary:

* Cloud hosting and infrastructure providers  
* Analytics and performance tools  
* Payment processors  
* Educational partners or institutions (where you have authorised sharing, e.g., school dashboards)

All third parties are required to sign Data Processing Agreements (or equivalent) and provide adequate safeguards to protect your data in line with the NDPA.

We do not share data with third parties for their own marketing purposes.

#### **7\. International Data Transfers**

Your data may be stored or processed outside Nigeria (for example, on secure cloud servers). When we transfer personal data internationally, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place, such as:

* Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)  
* Other NDPC-approved mechanisms  
* Adequacy decisions (where available)

We prioritise secure cloud providers with strong security standards.

#### **8\. Data Retention**

We retain your personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected or as required by law:

* Active accounts: retained for the duration of your relationship with us.  
* Inactive accounts: generally deleted or anonymised after 24 months of inactivity.  
* Children’s data: deleted upon valid request from a parent/guardian or when no longer needed.

You may request deletion of your data at any time (subject to legal retention obligations).

#### **9\. Your Rights as a Data Subject**

Under the NDPA, you have the following rights:

* Right to be informed  
* Right of access to your personal data  
* Right to rectification (correction of inaccurate data)  
* Right to erasure (“right to be forgotten”)  
* Right to restrict processing  
* Right to object to processing  
* Right to data portability  
* Right to withdraw consent (where processing is based on consent)  
* Right to lodge a complaint with the Nigeria Data Protection Commission (NDPC)

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at the details below. We will respond within one (1) month (or as required by law).

#### **10\. Data Security**

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data, including:

* Encryption (in transit and at rest where feasible)  
* Secure Socket Layer (SSL/TLS)  
* Role-based access controls  
* Regular security assessments and audits  
* Staff training on data protection

However, no system is completely secure. We encourage you to keep your login credentials confidential.
